Scenario 1 SQL: Claims Volume Analysis

Question: How would you identify trends in claim volumes over time to assist in resource allocation?

Below is the SQL Query

Step 1: I identified the columns needed for the analysis and do an outline of what my query would look like. Assuming I have columns year and month with the claims dates yyyy-mm-dd separated from the months.

  • -Getting Query Outline

SELECT

year(claim_date) AS "Claim Year",

month(claim_date) AS "claim_month",

count(claim_id) AS "Total_claims"

FROM claims

GROUP BY year(claim_date), MONTH(claim_date)

ORDER BY claim_year, claim_month;

Step 2: Narrowing the focus to specific year and month. Assuming there is a column that is labeled Year in the table and rows with dates yyyy-mm-dd and the same for month? There is also a column called claim_id.

Counting the claim_id will give each unique case and a total claims count. In the output there will be a column for year and month and total_claims.

Visualization: The results can be visualized using a line chart to show the trends over various years, such as a two-year span or a line chart showing claims for each month. The line chart showing the trends over time can identify the months or years when there were more or fewer claims.

  • -Narrowing focus to refine date and filter

SELECT

year(claim_date) AS "Claim_year",

month_name(claim_date) AS "Claim_month",

COUNT(claim_id) AS "Total_claims"

FROM claims

WHERE year(claim_date) BETWEEN 2011 AND 2013

GROUP BY year(claim_date), month(claim_date)

ORDER BY claim_year, claim_month;

Step 3: It would be even more comprehensive if the date would indicate they type of claims that were submitted. For this the query is optimized to show the type of claim.

  • -Refining focus to include claim type

SELECT

year(claim_date) AS "Claim_year",

month_name(claim_date) AS "Claim_month",

count(claim_id) AS "Total_claims",

claim_type AS “Claim_type

FROM claims

WHERE year(claim_date) BETWEEN 2011 AND 2013

AND claim_type = 'Flood'

GROUP BY year(claim_date), MONTH(claim_date), claim_type

ORDER BY claim_year, claim_month;

Scenario 1 Excel: Claims Volume Pivot Table and Visualization

Scenario 1: Claims Volume Analysis

In this scenario, I performed a Claims Volume Analysis to examine insurance claims based on their type and the corresponding claim amounts. The analysis utilizes pivot tables and charts to uncover trends in claims data, providing a clear understanding of claim volumes and amounts across different periods.

Approach:

  • Pivot Tables were created to organize claims by claim type and provide summary statistics such as the average, maximum, and minimum claim amounts. These calculations offer valuable insights into how claims are distributed and highlight potential anomalies or outliers.
  • Charts were incorporated to visualize the relationship between claim type and claim amount. The chart offers a clear and intuitive visual of claims data, making it easy to identify which claim types tend to have higher or lower claim amounts.
  • Filters were applied to the pivot table to allow for dynamic analysis, enabling the selection of specific claim types, periods, and claim amounts. This flexibility makes the analysis more interactive and responsive to different questions or business needs.

Scenario 2 Excel: Fraud Detection VLOOK UP / Conditional Formatting

Question:

How would you identify potentially fraudulent claims based on claim amounts exceeding a certain threshold?

This analysis focuses on detecting anomalies in insurance claims to flag potential fraud. I used historical claims data to compare current claims against a set threshold, determined by the average for each claim type. To achieve this, I applied advanced Excel techniques such as VLOOKUP, conditional formatting, and a nested IF AND OR formula. These methods helped to identify claims that deviate significantly from historical patterns and categorize them for further investigation.
