About the Data

This comprehensive dataset provides valuable insights into the growth patterns of various plants under different environmental conditions.

It includes detail data on the growth stages and development of the lettuce plant influenced by factors such as:

  • Temperature (0C)
  • Humidity (%)
  • pH Level
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) in ppm
  • Growth Days (number of days from sewn to fully grown)

Objective

What are the optimal conditions for successful lettuce growth?

What is the correlation between Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) is the combined content of all inorganic and organic substances dissolved in water, including salts, minerals, and metals.

Why TDS Matter?

Water Quality Indicator: High TDS levels can indicate contamination or the presence of excessive minerals and salts that affect growth or water usability.

Hydroponic systems: Too high or too low TDS levels can impact plant health, leading to nutrient deficiencies or toxicities.

Soil and Fertility: TDS tells whether the minerals in the soil are soluble. An excessive amount can lead to poor absorption and soil salinity, which in turn leads to poor crop yields.

Key Findings

Scatter plots showed that increased TDS levels did not have a positive impact on the growth rate, particularly during the germination stage.

Analysis revealed that a combination of moderate temperature, slightly acidic pH, and balanced TDS levels facilitated the most rapid growth during the early phases.

Growth rate consistency was observed across different stages, some variations of lettuce plants may experience increased growth in the mature stage.

Conclusion

Minimal Impact of TDS:

  • The scatter plot indicates that TDS is not a significant driver of lettuce growth. While TDS is present in the soil, the data suggests its influence on growth is minimal.
  • Growth occurs with or without TDS, and in many cases, there is no correlation between TDS levels and observable growth, suggesting that TDS is only necessary in small amounts.

Polynomial Trend Line Analysis:

  • The polynomial trend line on the data visualization shows that while TDS and growth follow parallel trends, they do not directly influence each other.
  • This implies that TDS may play a secondary or background role in plant growth, with stronger impacts likely coming from other factors such as temperature, pH, or additional nutrients.

Conclusion on TDS Presence:

  • The presence of TDS in the soil can be beneficial, but it is not a critical factor for successful lettuce growth according to this analysis.
  • Other environmental factors are likely more influential in determining the success of lettuce growth.
