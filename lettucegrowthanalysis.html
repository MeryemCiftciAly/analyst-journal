Lettuce Growth Dashboard | PBI

Lettuce Growth: A dashboard visualization of the growth of lettuce through growth stages

Objective & Motivation:

I chose lettuce as the focus for my first analysis because, despite being an often-overlooked ingredient, lettuce is essential to a great salad—and who doesn’t love a good salad? The idea struck me while preparing my own, and I became curious about what environmental factors most significantly contribute to its growth. With this analysis, I aim to uncover the optimal conditions for cultivating lettuce, with a particular focus on the role of Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), and share insights that could enhance growing practices.

This dashboard captures the growth patterns of lettuce (and other plants) under varying environmental conditions over time. It provides detailed insights into how factors like temperature, humidity, pH level, and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) influence plant development.

Data Fields:

  • Plant_ID: A unique identifier for each plant.
  • Date: The date when the observation was made.
  • Temperature (°C): Ambient temperature in degrees Celsius.
  • Humidity (%): Humidity level as a percentage.
  • TDS Value (ppm): Total Dissolved Solids measured in parts per million.
  • pH Level: The pH level of the growing environment.
  • Growth Days: The number of days from planting until the plant reached full growth.

Addressing Azure Limitations in My Power BI Dashboard

After creating and publishing my Power BI dashboard to the Microsoft web server, I encountered an issue: several interactive elements I had included weren’t supported. These elements, which depended on Azure’s backend capabilities, had to be removed to make the dashboard publishable. Here’s a breakdown of the problem and what it taught me:

Understanding the Role of Azure in Power BI

Azure’s Role:

Azure is Microsoft’s cloud computing platform that powers many advanced features in Power BI. Some interactive elements rely on Azure’s AI and machine learning services to function.

Unsupported Elements in My Dashboard:

  1. Q&A Box:
    • Purpose: The Q&A box allowed users to input natural language queries such as “What’s the highest pH level?” or “What’s the minimum temperature?”
    • Challenge: This feature uses natural language processing to interpret and dynamically query the dataset. It relies on AI services provided by Azure, which were not supported in the current publishing environment.
  1. Key Influencers Visual:
    • Purpose: This visual was designed to analyze the relationship between lettuce growth and factors like temperature and TDS, allowing users to select plant stages from a dropdown menu for detailed insights.
    • Challenge: The key influencers visual uses machine learning to identify which factors significantly impact outcomes, such as lettuce growth. Like the Q&A box, it required Azure-based AI capabilities that were unavailable in the publishable version of my dashboard.

What I Learned

This experience underscored the importance of understanding the dependencies of interactive features in modern BI tools. It also provided an opportunity to adapt my approach to ensure that my dashboard remains functional and accessible without these advanced elements.

Moving Forward

By resolving this issue, I not only improved the robustness of my Power BI solution but also gained valuable insights into the interplay between cloud services and interactive data visualization. This experience reinforces my commitment to continuous learning and adaptability—qualities I bring to every data analysis project.

Adapting the Dashboard After Addressing Azure Limitations

To work around the removal of Azure-dependent features, I focused on retaining key visual elements that effectively conveyed the story of my analysis. The final dashboard includes:

  • KPI Cards: Displaying average values for temperature, pH, humidity, and TDS, providing a quick snapshot of the essential metrics.
  • Normalized Clustered Column Charts: Offering balanced comparisons between measures with different units—such as Celsius for temperature and ppm for TDS—to ensure clarity in the data presentation.
  • Scatter Plot with Play Axis: Highlighting the progression of lettuce growth stages (seedling, germination, mature) over the observation period, effectively illustrating temporal trends.
  • Visual Cue: A graphic depicting soil, sun, and rain, which suggests the influence of additional environmental factors like sunlight and rainfall.

These elements collectively illustrate key metrics, the influence of environmental factors, and the dynamic stages of lettuce growth over time. They also hint at broader conditions affecting optimal growth, ensuring that the narrative of the analysis is both clear and comprehensive. I concluded that these features were sufficient to tell the story without needing further additions.
