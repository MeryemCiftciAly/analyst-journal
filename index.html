Immigration and Environment Analysis

This project examines the relationship between immigration and environmental factors to understand how migration patterns influence and are influenced by environmental conditions. The analysis will focus on identifying trends and correlations between immigration data and environmental variables such as air quality, climate change, and resource availability. By exploring these connections, the project aims to provide insights into how immigration affects environmental sustainability and vice versa.

Expected Impact:

The findings from this analysis are expected to highlight key areas where immigration impacts environmental conditions and where environmental changes may drive migration patterns. The recommendations will offer strategies for policymakers to address environmental challenges associated with immigration and promote sustainable practices that benefit both immigrant communities and the environment.
