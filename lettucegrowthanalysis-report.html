Uncovering Insights: An Analysis of Lettuce Growth and Environmental Impacts

Dataset: An Environmental Data Analysis | September 24 (Fabor, 2024), accessed via Kaggle

Key Question

What is the correlation between Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) and lettuce growth?

TDS = Combined content of inorganic and organic substances in water (e.g., salts, minerals).

Summary of Findings

Minimal Impact of TDS:

  • TDS levels show minimal influence on lettuce growth.
  • Growth patterns do not strongly correlate with TDS concentrations.

Visual Insight

Scatter Plot Analysis:

  • Trend line: Indicates parallel movement between TDS and growth but lacks a direct relationship.
  • Suggests that TDS plays a secondary role compared to temperature and nutrients.

Lettuce Growth Analysis Insights

TDS and Growth

  • Minimal Impact of TDS: Scatter plot indicates TDS has little influence on lettuce growth. Growth occurs with or without significant TDS levels.
  • Correlation Findings: No clear correlation between TDS levels and growth. Small amounts may support plant health but are not the main driver of growth.
  • Polynomial Trendline Insight: Suggests TDS and growth are somewhat related but don’t directly impact each other.
  • More Influential Factors: Likely include temperature, pH, and other nutrients.
  • Conclusion: TDS is not critical for growth success. Focus should shift to more impactful environmental conditions.

pH and Growth

  • Vertical Alignment: Growth often occurs at specific pH levels with some variability.
  • Horizontal Stability: Growth remains consistent at certain pH levels, indicating stable conditions.
  • Outliers:
    • Growth at High pH: Occurs away from the trend line, suggesting pH isn’t always a limiting factor.
    • pH Without Growth: Some high pH values do not support growth.

    Conclusion: The pH-growth relationship is complex. While pH influences growth, it doesn’t align directly, implying other factors may be significant.


Temperature and Growth

  • Optimal Growth at Moderate Temperatures: Polynomial trendlines show lettuce grows best within a moderate temperature range.
  • Slower Growth at High Temperatures: Higher temperatures lead to slower growth, confirmed by the temperature trendline above the growth day trendline.
  • Non-linear Relationship: A slight upward curve in normalized temperature data indicates that moderate temperatures promote faster growth, but extremely high temperatures hinder it.

Conclusion:

  • Moderate temperatures are critical for optimal growth.
  • Higher temperatures lead to slower growth.
  • Threshold effect: Too much heat negatively impacts growth.

Humidity and Growth

  • Optimal Growth at Moderate Humidity: The coiling pattern shows lettuce thrives in environments with moderate humidity, not too high or low.
  • Threshold Effect: Low humidity leads to minimal growth; growth begins once humidity reaches a certain level.
  • Polynomial Trendline: Supports the idea that moderate humidity promotes growth, but extreme levels disrupt it.
  • Balanced Range: A specific humidity range is ideal for lettuce growth.

Conclusion:

  • Moderate humidity is crucial for optimal growth.
  • Too much or too little humidity hinders growth.
  • Data doesn’t specify air vs. soil humidity, but moderate levels are key.
