Lettuce Growth Analysis Dashboard

Original Dashboard Design

After creating and publishing the dashboard to the Power BI web server, I encountered an issue: some interactive elements I had included weren’t supported. These elements, tied to Azure services, had to be removed to make the dashboard publishable.

Understanding the Problem With Azure

Azure is Microsoft’s cloud computing platform that supports a variety of tools and services, including some advanced features in Power BI. The unsupported elements in my dashboard—such as the Q&A box and the Key Influencers visual—likely relied on Azure's backend capabilities.

  • Q&A Box: My Q&A box was for the user to input and ask questions such as "What’s the highest pH level?" or "What’s the minimum temperature?”
    • The problem: The Q&A uses natural language processing to understand user questions and query the data dynamically. This feature relies on certain AI services within Azure.

  • Key Influencers Visual: This visual was to analyze the relationship between growth and other factors such as temperature, and TDS when the user chooses the plant stages from a dropdown menu.
    • The problem: The key influencer visual uses machine learning to analyze patterns in the dataset and identify factors that significantly influence outcomes, such as lettuce growth. This too likely required Azure-based AI features to process the data.

Published Dashboard

Brief Analysis Over: This analysis explores the effects of temperature, TDS, moisture, and pH on lettuce growth, with a focus on identifying the most influential factor: TDS.

The questions asked:

What are the optimal conditions for successful lettuce growth?

What is the correlation between Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) and the growth of lettuce?

Dashboard Adaptation

When publishing my dashboard to the Power BI web server, I discovered that Azure-dependent features like the Q&A box and Key Influencers chart were unsupported. To adapt, I focused on retaining key elements that effectively conveyed the story of my analysis.

The final dashboard included:

  • KPI Cards: Displaying averages for temperature, pH, humidity, and TDS.
  • Normalized Clustered Column Charts: Providing a balanced comparison of measures with differing units, such as Celsius for temperature and ppm for TDS.
  • Scatter Plot with Play Axis: Highlighting the growth stages of lettuce (seedling, germination, mature) over observation days.
  • Visual Cue: A graphic of soil, sun, and rain, suggesting additional environmental factors like sunlight and rainfall.

These elements illustrated the averages, the influence of key metrics, and the stages of lettuce growth over time, all while hinting at broader factors affecting optimal growth conditions. I concluded that the remaining features were sufficient to tell the story without requiring further additions.

Lessons Learned

This experience taught me the importance of reviewing documentation on publishing features to anticipate potential limitations. When unexpected challenges arose, I adapted by focusing on the core story my dashboard told and evaluating whether adjustments were necessary. Ultimately, I learned that simplicity, when effective, can be more impactful than adding redundant elements.
